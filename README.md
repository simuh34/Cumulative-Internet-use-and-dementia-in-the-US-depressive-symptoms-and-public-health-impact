# Cumulative-Internet-use-and-dementia-in-the-US-depressive-symptoms-and-public-health-impact

This project investigates whether cumulative Internet use is associated with the risk of dementia among US middle-aged and older adults, how depressive symptoms mediate this association, and the potential public health impacts of increasing Internet use. In this study of 8,493 US adults aged 50 years and older, greater cumulative Internet use was inversely associated with a lower risk of dementia, an association partially mediated by depressive symptoms; furthermore, simulated interventions indicated that shifting the population to higher Internet use could avert 0.56 million to 1.65 million dementia cases and save $18.17 billion to $53.76 billion from 2010 to 2021. These findings suggest that increasing Internet use, particularly by supporting mental health, may serve as a cost-effective public health strategy to reduce dementia risk, while delivering substantial clinical and economic benefits.

# Requirement
R version 4.5 or higher.

# Scripts descriptions
- The file "HRS 01 Data Extraction.R" extract raw data from HRS survey databases for subsequent analysis.
- The file "HRS 02 Data Cleaning.R" conduct data cleaning on the extracted datasets to generate clean, analysis-ready datasets.
- The file "HRS 03 Dementia.R", outline the methodology employed to process and analyze data related to dementia in this project.
- The file "HRS 04 Mediation.R" conduct mediation analysis.
- The file "HRS 05 Intervention.R" is designed to simulate intervention.
